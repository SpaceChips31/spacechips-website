Operator Profile

Chips
Driven by logic, caffeine, and side quests.

I’m a programmer and hardware technician based in Italy — nerdy by default, a little weird by design, and methodical by necessity. By day, I work with code and components. By night, I dive into 3D modeling, play the drums, prototype tools I may never finish, or just follow whatever rabbit hole my brain decides to chase that day.

I created Spacechips to share the things that fascinate me — discoveries, small breakthroughs, strange ideas — and to hopefully build a space where people like me (and unlike me) feel welcome. I love the idea of being a kind of “tech mentor” for those who feel new to it all. I’ve been that person, looking for clear guides and finding only scattered docs or noise. So I decided to write the kind of content I wish I had when I started.

Spacechips is also a tool for me — a way to keep myself grounded, especially when ADHD pulls me in 20 directions. It helps me stay focused, motivated, and connected to what I build.

Most of what I share comes from tools I use in my daily work or experiments: Linux setups, hardware builds, visual tricks, and code patterns I’ve tested myself. Some of it might turn into tutorials, some into creative renders or blog entries powered by obsession and a good playlist.

⚙️ Things I like (besides code):

  • Music and drumming — loud rhythms make my brain happy.
  • Puzzle kits, robot building, and DIY electronics.
  • Sci-fi films, quiet walks with my dog, and that one Spotify playlist I keep looping like a ritual.

🧭 What I care about:

  • Making complex things easier to understand.
  • Being honest, curious, and always a bit offbeat.
  • Helping people go from “I don’t get this” to “I’ve got this.”
